# USCIS Regulatory Framework

## Overview

This document provides the complete regulatory framework for O-1, P-1, and EB-1A visa categories. All petition arguments should be grounded in these regulatory citations.

---

## 1. O-1 Visa Regulations

### 1.1 Statutory Authority

**INA Section 101(a)(15)(O)**

The O nonimmigrant classification is for aliens who:
- **O-1A:** Have extraordinary ability in sciences, education, business, or athletics
- **O-1B (Arts):** Have extraordinary ability in the arts
- **O-1B (Film/TV):** Have extraordinary achievement in motion picture or television
- **O-2:** Are accompanying and assisting O-1 aliens
- **O-3:** Are spouses or children of O-1 or O-2 aliens

### 1.2 O-1A Evidentiary Criteria

**8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)**

To establish extraordinary ability in sciences, education, business, or athletics, the petition must include evidence of sustained national or international acclaim demonstrating achievements recognized in the field. Such evidence shall include **at least three** of the following:

| Criterion | Regulatory Citation | Brief Description |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **(A) Awards** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(A) | Receipt of nationally or internationally recognized prizes or awards for excellence |
| **(B) Membership** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(B) | Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievements as judged by recognized experts |
| **(C) Published Material** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(C) | Published material in professional or major trade publications or major media about the alien |
| **(D) Judging** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(D) | Participation as a judge of the work of others in same or allied field |
| **(E) Original Contributions** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(E) | Original scientific, scholarly, or business-related contributions of major significance |
| **(F) Authorship** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(F) | Authorship of scholarly articles in professional journals or major media |
| **(G) Critical Employment** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(G) | Employment in critical or essential capacity for distinguished organizations |
| **(H) High Salary** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(H) | Commands high salary or remuneration compared to others |

### 1.3 O-1B Evidentiary Criteria (Arts)

**8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)**

For extraordinary ability in the arts, the petition must include **at least three** of:

| Criterion | Regulatory Citation | Brief Description |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **(A) Lead Participation** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(A) | Performed as lead or starring participant in distinguished productions |
| **(B) National/International Recognition** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(B) | Achieved recognition as shown by critical reviews or publications |
| **(C) Critical Role** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(C) | Performed in lead/critical role for distinguished organizations |
| **(D) Commercial Success** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(D) | Record of major commercial or critically acclaimed successes |
| **(E) Significant Recognition** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(E) | Received recognition from organizations, critics, or experts |
| **(F) High Salary** | 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(F) | Commands high salary or substantial remuneration |

### 1.4 O-1B Evidentiary Criteria (Motion Picture/Television)

**8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(v)**

For extraordinary achievement in motion picture or television, **at least three** of:

| Criterion | Brief Description |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **(A) Nominations/Awards** | Nominated for or received significant national/international awards |
| **(B) Critical Employment** | Employment requiring exceptional skill |
| **(C) Critical Reviews** | Published materials about the alien |
| **(D) Commercial/Critical Success** | Major commercial or critical success |
| **(E) Significant Recognition** | Recognition from experts |
| **(F) High Salary** | High salary or substantial remuneration |

### 1.5 O-1 Advisory Opinion Requirements

**8 CFR 214.2(o)(5)**

- **O-1A (arts/athletics):** Advisory opinion from peer group or labor organization
- **O-1B:** Advisory opinion from labor organization with expertise in beneficiary's area
- **O-2:** Advisory opinion required for arts/athletics

**Relevant Labor Organizations:**
- SAG-AFTRA (actors)
- AFM (musicians)
- AGMA (musical artists)
- AEA (stage actors)
- WGA (writers)
- DGA (directors)

## 2. P-1 Visa Regulations

### 2.1 Statutory Authority

**INA Section 101(a)(15)(P)**

The P nonimmigrant classification is for:
- **P-1A:** Internationally recognized individual athletes
- **P-1B:** Internationally recognized entertainment groups
- **P-1S:** Essential support personnel
- **P-2:** Artists/entertainers under reciprocal exchange programs
- **P-3:** Artists/entertainers in culturally unique programs

### 2.2 P-1A Individual Athlete Criteria

**8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)**

For individual athletes, the petition must include **at least two** of:

| Criterion | Regulatory Citation | Brief Description |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **(A) Prior Major League** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(A) | Significant participation in prior season with major U.S. sports league |
| **(B) International Competition** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(B) | Significant participation in international competition with national team |
| **(C) Intercollegiate** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(C) | Significant participation in U.S. college/university intercollegiate competition |
| **(D) Official Statement** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(D) | Written statement from major league or governing body official |
| **(E) Media/Expert Statement** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(E) | Written statement from sports media or recognized expert |
| **(F) International Ranking** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(F) | Evidence of international ranking |
| **(G) Significant Honor** | 8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(G) | Evidence of significant honor or award in the sport |

### 2.3 P-1B Entertainment Group Criteria

**8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(iii)**

For entertainment groups:
- Must demonstrate international recognition as a group
- At least 75% of members must have been performing with group for at least one year

**Evidence may include:**
- Nominations for significant awards
- Critical reviews or other published materials
- Evidence of lead billing
- Major commercial or critical successes
- Significant recognition from experts
- High salary or remuneration

### 2.4 P-1 Consultation Requirements

**8 CFR 214.2(p)(6)**

- Consultation required with appropriate labor organization
- Must address nature of work and whether alien/group qualifies
- No consultation required if petitioner is a labor organization

## 3. EB-1A Regulations

### 3.1 Statutory Authority

**INA Section 203(b)(1)(A)**

Priority workers: Aliens with extraordinary ability in sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics demonstrated by sustained national or international acclaim.

### 3.2 Evidentiary Criteria

**8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)**

The petition must include **at least three** of the following (or one-time achievement):

| Criterion | Regulatory Citation | Brief Description |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **(i) Awards** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(i) | Lesser nationally/internationally recognized prizes or awards |
| **(ii) Membership** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(ii) | Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievements |
| **(iii) Published Material** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(iii) | Published material about the alien in professional/major publications |
| **(iv) Judging** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(iv) | Participation as judge of others' work |
| **(v) Original Contributions** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(v) | Original contributions of major significance |
| **(vi) Scholarly Articles** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(vi) | Authorship of scholarly articles |
| **(vii) Artistic Exhibitions** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(vii) | Display of work at artistic exhibitions or showcases |
| **(viii) Critical Role** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(viii) | Leading or critical role for distinguished organizations |
| **(ix) High Salary** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(ix) | High salary compared to others in field |
| **(x) Commercial Success** | 8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(x) | Commercial successes in performing arts |

### 3.3 One-Time Achievement

**8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)**

A one-time achievement (major internationally recognized award such as Nobel Prize, Oscar, Olympic medal) can satisfy the extraordinary ability requirement without meeting three criteria.

### 3.4 Comparable Evidence

**8 CFR 204.5(h)(4)**

If the criteria do not readily apply to the beneficiary's occupation, the petitioner may submit comparable evidence to establish eligibility.

**Key Requirements:**
- Must explain why criteria don't apply
- Must show comparable evidence is truly equivalent
- Must demonstrate same level of achievement

## 4. Key Definitions

### 4.1 "Extraordinary Ability" (O-1A and EB-1A)

**Regulatory Definition:**
> A level of expertise indicating that the individual is one of the small percentage who have risen to the very top of their field of endeavor.

**Key Elements:**
- "Small percentage" - not the majority
- "Very top" - highest level of achievement
- "Field of endeavor" - the specific professional area

### 4.2 "Extraordinary Ability in the Arts" (O-1B)

**Regulatory Definition:**
> Distinction means a high level of achievement in the arts evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition substantially above that ordinarily encountered.

**Key Elements:**
- "High level" - not average or ordinary
- "Substantially above" - clear differentiation from peers
- Lower standard than O-1A "extraordinary ability"

### 4.3 "Extraordinary Achievement" (O-1B Film/TV)

**Regulatory Definition:**
> A very high level of accomplishment in the motion picture or television industry evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition significantly above that ordinarily encountered.

### 4.4 "Internationally Recognized" (P-1)

**Regulatory Definition:**
> Having a high level of achievement in a field evidenced by a degree of skill and recognition substantially above that ordinarily encountered, to the extent that such achievement is renowned, leading, or well-known in more than one country.

## 5. Burden of Proof

### 5.1 Preponderance of Evidence Standard

**Matter of Chawathe, 25 I&N Dec. 369 (AAO 2005)**

The petitioner must establish eligibility by a **preponderance of the evidence**, meaning:
- The claim is "probably true"
- The claim is "more likely than not"
- Not certainty, but probability

### 5.2 Kazarian Two-Step Analysis

**Kazarian v. USCIS, 596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010)**

| Step | Analysis | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Step 1** | Determine whether evidence meets at least three criteria | Initial threshold |
| **Step 2** | If Step 1 satisfied, conduct final merits determination | Assess totality of evidence |

**Step 1: Criteria Counting**
- Review evidence for each claimed criterion
- Determine if regulatory requirement is met
- Need at least three criteria satisfied

**Step 2: Final Merits**
- Does totality of evidence demonstrate extraordinary ability?
- Is there sustained national or international acclaim?
- Is beneficiary among the small percentage at the very top?

## 6. Key Precedent Decisions

### 6.1 Binding Precedent

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Matter of Chawathe** | 25 I&N Dec. 369 (AAO 2005) | Preponderance of evidence standard |
| **Kazarian v. USCIS** | 596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010) | Two-step analysis framework |
| **Matter of Dhanasar** | 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016) | National interest waiver framework |

### 6.2 Key Principles from Precedent

**From Kazarian:**
- Meeting criteria alone is insufficient
- Final merits determination required
- Totality of evidence must demonstrate extraordinary ability

**From Chawathe:**
- Petitioner bears burden of proof
- Standard is preponderance (more likely than not)
- Doubt is resolved against petitioner

## 9. Citation Guidelines for Petitions

### 9.1 Proper Regulatory Citation Format

**When Citing Criteria:**
```
Pursuant to 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(A), [BENEFICIARY] qualifies under
the awards criterion based on [his/her] receipt of [AWARD].
```

**When Citing Definitions:**
```
As defined in the regulations, "extraordinary ability" means
a level of expertise indicating that the individual is one of the
small percentage who have risen to the very top of their field of
endeavor. See 8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(ii).
```

**When Citing Precedent:**
```
Applying the two-step framework established in Kazarian v. USCIS,
596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010), [BENEFICIARY] satisfies both the
initial evidentiary requirements and the final merits determination.
```

### 9.2 Common Citation Errors to Avoid

| Error | Correct Approach |
|-------|------------------|
| Citing wrong visa category criteria | Verify correct CFR section |
| Misquoting regulatory language | Quote directly from regulation |
| Ignoring final merits requirement | Always address Step 2 |
| Conflating O-1A with O-1B | Clearly distinguish standards |
| Missing case citations | Include relevant precedent |
