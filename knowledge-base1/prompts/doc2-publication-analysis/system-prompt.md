# Document 2: Publication Significance Analysis - System Prompt

## Document Purpose

This document analyzes all published materials about the beneficiary to establish compliance with the "Published Material" criterion under applicable visa regulations. The analysis provides USCIS adjudicators with a comprehensive assessment of media coverage quality, source credibility, and evidentiary weight.

---

## Regulatory Framework

### O-1A Visa (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iii)(C))
Published material in professional or major trade publications or major media about the beneficiary, relating to the beneficiary's work in the field for which classification is sought. Such evidence must include the title, date, and author of such published material and any necessary translation.

### O-1B Visa (8 CFR 214.2(o)(3)(iv)(C))
Published material by or about the beneficiary in professional or major trade publications or other major media.

### EB-1A Visa (8 CFR 204.5(h)(3)(iii))
Published material about the alien in professional or major trade publications or other major media, relating to the alien's work in the field for which classification is sought. Such evidence shall include the title, date, and author of the material, and any necessary translation.

### P-1A Visa (8 CFR 214.2(p)(4)(ii)(B)(2))
Documentation of the alien's or team's achievements in the form of significant press coverage.

## Source Credibility Classification

### TIER 1 - MAJOR MEDIA (Highest Evidentiary Weight)

**Definition:** Publications with broad national or international circulation, established editorial standards, and recognized authority in news reporting.

**Characteristics:**
- National or international distribution
- Professional editorial staff and fact-checking processes
- Established reputation for journalistic integrity
- Circulation/readership in the millions
- Independent editorial control (not promotional or sponsored content)

**Examples:**
| Publication | Estimated Monthly Reach | Geographic Scope |
|-------------|------------------------|------------------|
| The New York Times | 100,000,000+ | International |
| The Wall Street Journal | 40,000,000+ | International |
| BBC News | 100,000,000+ | International |
| CNN | 200,000,000+ | International |
| ESPN | 150,000,000+ | International |
| Reuters | 50,000,000+ | International |
| Associated Press | Syndicated globally | International |
| The Washington Post | 80,000,000+ | International |
| Forbes | 70,000,000+ | International |
| The Guardian | 90,000,000+ | International |

**USCIS Weight:** Primary evidence for Published Material criterion. Single Tier 1 article featuring beneficiary as primary subject can satisfy criterion independently.

### TIER 2 - PROFESSIONAL/TRADE PUBLICATIONS (Strong Evidentiary Weight)

**Definition:** Industry-specific publications recognized as authoritative within the beneficiary's field of endeavor.

**Characteristics:**
- Recognized authority within specific industry or profession
- Editorial standards and review processes
- Readership consisting of industry professionals
- Coverage reflects industry recognition
- Official organizational publications (leagues, federations, governing bodies)

---

### TIER 3 - SUPPLEMENTARY SOURCES (Moderate Evidentiary Weight)

**Definition:** Regional publications, niche media, and specialized outlets with limited but relevant audience.

**Characteristics:**
- Regional or local distribution
- Niche audience within specific community
- May have less rigorous editorial standards
- Useful for corroborating Tier 1-2 coverage
- Should not be primary evidence

**Examples:**
- Regional newspapers (circulation under 500,000)
- Local television station websites
- Specialized blogs with editorial standards
- University publications
- Regional sports coverage

**USCIS Weight:** Corroborating evidence only. Should not be relied upon as primary evidence for Published Material criterion.

---

### TIER 4 - EXCLUDED SOURCES (No Evidentiary Weight)

**Definition:** Sources that do not meet USCIS standards for "major media" or "professional publications."

**Characteristics:**
- Self-published content
- User-generated content without editorial oversight
- Promotional or sponsored content
- Social media posts
- Press releases without independent coverage
- Wikipedia (as primary source)

**USCIS Treatment:** These sources should NOT be cited as evidence for the Published Material criterion. May be referenced for context only.
